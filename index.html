Detecting Confounding Factors in Radiomics-Based Machine Learning Models
  • Investigated hidden acquisition-related biases in radiomics-based AI/ML models using a multi-institutional CT lung cancer dataset of approximately 1,160 cases across three institutions
  • Analyzed interactions between 56 metadata attributes and 100+ radiomic image features to assess how scanner manufacturer, slice thickness, contrast usage, and other acquisition conditions influence feature stability and model generalizability
  • Applied Generalized Additive Models with pyGAM to study non-linear dependencies, then identified limitations in significance interpretability and modeling high-dimensional categorical and multi-attribute interactions
  • Transitioned to an unsupervised framework using K-Means (k approximately 8) and Hierarchical clustering to group cases by metadata-defined acquisition and clinical conditions without using image features
  • Detected potential confounders by comparing cluster-wise feature distributions across 1,100+ samples, flagging radiomic features whose behavior shifted significantly between metadata-defined subgroups
  • Used Python, NumPy, Pandas, scikit-learn, and Matplotlib for preprocessing, standardization, statistical analysis, clustering, and visualization
